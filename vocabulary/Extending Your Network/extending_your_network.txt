4→Firewall|A device within a network responsible for determining what traffic is allowed to enter and exit; can permit or deny traffic based on source, destination, port, and protocol; can be hardware or software (e.g. Snort).|security device
5→Stateful Firewall|A firewall that inspects the entire connection context rather than individual packets; blocks a whole device if bad behavior is detected; more resource-intensive but more intelligent.|firewall type
6→Stateless Firewall|A firewall that uses a static set of rules to evaluate individual packets independently; less resource-intensive but less intelligent; useful for handling large volumes of traffic such as DDoS attacks.|firewall type
